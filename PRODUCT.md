# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Coffee brewers standing at a counter with a scale, kettle, or cold-brew vessel, contributors adding new recipes, and curious readers comparing methods. They want structured brew parameters — ratio, dose, temperature, duration, and sequence — without opening raw YAML files or hunting through recipe-blog prose.

## Product Purpose

brew-recipes turns a static YAML recipe database into a readable web surface. Success means people can quickly find a recipe by language and brew method, inspect practical parameters, follow each brew step, and trace source attribution.

## Brand Personality

Quiet, precise, archival. The product should feel careful and literate without making recipe lookup feel fragile or decorative.

## Anti-references

Avoid coffee-shop browns, latte art decoration, card grids, marketing hero sections, and recipe-blog clutter. The UI treats structured brew data as the primary content; it should never bury ratio, dose, or pour phases inside decorative prose. Rows and rules, not cards and shadows.

## Design Principles

- Make the YAML legible as a calm tool.
- Treat source attribution and phases as first-class content.
- Keep browsing fast, low-friction, and mobile-readable.
- Use atmosphere sparingly; readability wins every tradeoff.
- Preserve the multilingual archive structure instead of hiding it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA contrast, keyboard-accessible navigation, clear focus states, reduced-motion support, and responsive layouts that avoid horizontal scrolling for long recipe names or translated text.
